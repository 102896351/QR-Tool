What Makes a Great Free QR Generator in 2025?

Not all free QR tools are created equal. We evaluated each on these seven criteria:

1. Privacy — does it log your data?

Many free QR generators track the contents of every code you create and the scans that happen later. For a personal event QR code, that's not a big deal. For a business, it can leak customer data. Best in class: generators that don't store your data and don't require signup.

2. Customization — can you brand it?

Black-and-white codes are fine for utility use. For marketing, you want to add your logo, match your brand colors, and adjust the corner shapes. The best free tools offer at least color and logo upload without forcing a paid plan.

3. Output quality

Vector SVG, high-resolution PNG, and PDF print files. If you need a QR code on a billboard, you want SVG. If it's for a business card, high-DPI PNG works. JPEG is a deal-breaker — never use JPEG for a QR code.

4. Static vs. dynamic

Static codes encode the destination directly. They never expire, don't need a server, and work forever. Dynamic codes use a short URL that points to a server-side redirect, so the destination can be changed later. Dynamic is convenient but introduces a single point of failure.

5. Format support

URL, plain text, WiFi, vCard, email, phone, SMS, geo coordinates, calendar events. The more formats, the more use cases you cover with one tool.

6. Watermark and forced branding

Some free tools force a small "powered by" logo in the corner. That's fine for casual use but disqualifying for branded business materials.

7. Speed and UX

How many clicks from landing on the page to a downloaded QR code? The best tools get you there in under 30 seconds with no signup wall.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are free QR code generators really free?

Most are, but read the fine print. "Free" usually means static codes with limited customization. Dynamic codes, scan analytics, bulk generation, and team features are typically behind paid plans. Watch out for tools that brand your code with their logo on the free tier.

Do QR codes expire?

Static QR codes never expire. They encode the destination directly. Dynamic QR codes expire if the service shuts down or your subscription lapses. For long-term use (signage, packaging), always use static codes.

Can I track scans on a free QR code?

Not directly. To get scan analytics, you need a dynamic code that goes through a server. Most free tools don't offer this. If you need analytics, set up a free Bitly link and encode the Bitly URL as a static QR code — you'll get scan counts in your Bitly dashboard.

What resolution should I use for printing?

For screen: 300×300 pixels is enough. For print: use vector SVG and let the printer scale it. If you must use PNG, aim for 1000×1000 pixels at minimum. Avoid JPEG — it introduces artifacts that can break scannability.
